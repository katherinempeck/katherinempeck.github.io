About

I am a PhD student in archaeology at the University of New Mexico. My research focuses on human-environmental interactions, in particular using the framework of historical ecology to study ancient agricultural strategies. I use geoarchaeological and geospatial methods to examine questions like:

  • How do natural factors constrain agricultural productivity?
  • How do people overcome natural landscape limitations using agricultural strategies (like irrigation)?
  • How did ancient agricultural practices impact the environment?
  • How can studying ancient agricultural practices be useful for modern communities?

My current research focuses on a network of dryland fields in South Kohala, Hawaiʻi Island.
